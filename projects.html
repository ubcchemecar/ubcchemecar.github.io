Projects

Our most important events of the year are the annual AIChE Chem-E-Car competitions. However, we embark on several STEM-related side projects throughout the year with a focus on energy and electrochemistry. This page lists our plans for 2016-2017. Have some good ideas? We'd like to hear from you: info@ubcchemecar.com

Technical Resources

Chem-E-Car

AIChE's annual Chem-E-Car Competition® engages college students in designing and constructing a car powered by a chemical energy source, that will safely carry a specified load over a given distance and stop.

Project Description

This is our main project and where we focus the majority of our resources and manpower on. Our team decides on a power source and stopping mechanism some time in the summer and starts planning and designing the car in preparation for the AIChE Pacific Northwest Regionals Competition in April. If we pass the qualifying rounds, we compete in the AIChE National Competition in November later in the year. We will be representing UBC in the 2016 AIChE Nationals in San Francisco.

Rechargeable Batteries

Project Description

All of our past Chem-E-Cars have been powered by either fuel cells or primary cells (non-rechargeable batteries). We are attempting to research and construct some low-cost rechargeable batteries, both as a standalone project and a possible power source for our next Chem-E-Car. Our team is currently looking into Zn-Nickel flow cells, Li-ion batteries and alkaline cells.

Potentiostat

Project Description

We're are attempting to construct our own potentiostat, a device used to run some cool electrochemical experiments including catalysis studies and water quality analysis. Our team is currently studying papers for open source designs like DStat and CheapStat and attempting to reconstruct their hardware.

Outreach Programs

Project Description

We regularly receive invitations to STEM outreach programs from various organizations. To increase engagement with our audience, we'd like to construct some interesting science demonstration units that we can use in our presentations. Our team is looking into ideas based on chemical engineering and electrochemistry concepts.
