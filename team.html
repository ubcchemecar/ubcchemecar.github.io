Team Description

Administration Team

The administration team handles the day-to-day operations of the team and ensures that all projects are running smoothly.

Team Responsibilities

The administration team is responsible for:

  • Finances and Sponsorship: tracking team expenses and item orders, preparing proposals for internal funding applications such as the UBC Applied Science PAF and Shell Engineering Fund and soliciting external funding from corporate sponsors.
  • Logistics: coordinating with UBC and CHBE administrators for outreach events, workspace access and travel preparations, overseeing orientation and training for new members and organizing team socials.
  • Digital Communications: managing all software-based projects and web assets including the official website and social media presence, handling the team mailing list and email service and providing content creation and design strategy for presentation slides, posters and all marketing materials.
  • Lab Management: ensuring workspace safety and organization during meetings, tracking lab inventory, overseeing equipment maintenance and chemical storage and supervising all team members for proper PPE compliance.

Battery

The battery team builds the power sources for the car and tries not to blow up the lab while doing their experiments.

Team Description

Battery Team

The battery team is reponsible for constructing a device that powers the car. Every year, the battery team conducts research to compile a list of interesting and feasible electrochemical reactions. After selecting a reaction, the team designs and builds the battery casing which is used in battery testing experiments. Past projects include: zinc-air batteries, magnesium-air batteries, hydrogen fuel cells and pressurized cylinders.

Team Projects

Ongoing projects:

  • First attempt at making Li-ion batteries
  • Improve current Zn-air battery design using a gelling agent
  • Research and construction of a rechargeable Zn-air battery
